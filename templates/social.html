Chiang Kai-Shek Memorial Hall

This remarkable memorial hall was built in 1980 to memorize Chaing Kai-Shek, the former President of Taiwan. The memorial hall is in blue, white and red, colors of Taiwanese flag, representing independence, equality and universal love. The hall occupies a large area with a heritage display room and auditorium offering historical exhibitions and some fabulous performances. Moreover, what really impresses people is the solem rifle twirling exchange ceremony among soldiers.

Official website: http://www.cksmh.gov.tw/eng/index.php

National Palace Museum

The National Palace Museum is the world's largest collection of priceless artifacts, objects which span China’s 5000 yearlong history, which has made themselves the greatest and invaluable treasure house regarding Chinese art on earth. Many of the artifacts were once a part of the Chinese Imperial Collection, which was begun over 1000 years ago in the early Sung Dynasty. The items amounts to nearly 700,000 pieces ranging from delicately carved jade ornaments pottery, porcelain, as well as bronze, sculpture, embroidery, calligraphy and paintings, each piece is a true curio.There is also the lovely Zhishan Farden just right ahead. It was planned and construct based on one well-known painting in Chinese history in order to show the best part of elegant beauty.

Official website: http://www.npm.gov.tw/

Lung-Shan Temple

The Lungshan Temple is probably what comes to mind when you think of a Chinese temple, where you can experience the local Chinese culture for worships. It was rebuilt in 1920 and is incredibly well preserved and maintained. Located in Mengjia, the most prosperous area in the early Taiwanese history, Lungshan temple attracts people by its featured cornices, symbolic sculptures, traditional markets, calligraphies and fortune tellers. It is with amazing carving and painting on every surface of the temple from its 42 pillars to the painted walls. Besides, there is a place called Snake Alley where you can see the snake handlers dealing with those fierce snakes and serve up them as dinner. Lungshan Temple is the best choice for you to experience the traditional lifestyle andwitness the piety of believers.

You will also have chance to check out Taipei 101, the Yanminshan National Park Area and the Presidential House.

Banquet at Shi-Yan Cultural Restaurant

Shi-Yan Cultural Restaurant could be described as one of Taipei's premier examples of that culinary trend for its use of traditional, local ingredients and styles and prepared to appeal to today’s increasingly health-conscious and refined Taiwanese palate. Meals are relatively low in oil and salt and have plenty of vegetables.

A special mountainside retreat that's usually only found in nostalgic descriptions of a long-gone era. But now, Shi-Yan Culture Restaurant refines this vision and achieve a kind of cultural and culinary nirvana that exists in harmony with the surrounding hills and forests for you. A place for your healthy and soul.
